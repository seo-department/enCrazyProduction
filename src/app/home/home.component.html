Key Federal Acts Affecting US Online Gambling:

  • The Wire Act of 1961: Initially targeting sports betting over phone lines, its interpretation for online gambling has evolved. While it primarily pertains to sports betting, its implications for interstate online casino operations have been debated.
  • The Unlawful Internet Gambling Enforcement Act (UIGEA) of 2006: This law primarily targets financial institutions, prohibiting them from processing transactions related to unlawful internet gambling. It does not outlaw online gambling itself, but makes it harder for illegal operators to move money. It implicitly pushes states to regulate if they want legal online gambling.

Online casino gaming is growing rapidly across the United States, but full-scale iGaming is currently legal in just seven states. Each of these markets has developed unique legislation, tax structures, and performance metrics—shaping a diverse and evolving legal landscape for digital gambling.

Delaware: The Original Online Gambling Pioneer

Delaware was the first U.S. state to legalize iGaming back in 2012. Despite its small population, it remains a consistent revenue contributor with a well-rounded portfolio of online casino, poker, lottery, bingo, and sports betting. In October 2024, the iGaming revenue hit $6.7 million in Delaware, showcasing its long-standing stability in the space.

New Jersey: A Mature and Lucrative iGaming Market

Since going live in 2013, New Jersey has become one of the most profitable and regulated iGaming states in the country. It generated $213.6 million in October 2024 alone, with online casinos outperforming land-based properties. Top operators include Golden Nugget, Resorts (DraftKings), and BetMGM. The state’s framework delivers reliable tax contributions, with $31.9 million in iGaming taxes reported in October 2024.

Michigan: Fast-Growth Leader in Online Casino Revenue

Launched in 2021, Michigan has rapidly grown into a top-tier iGaming hub, setting a monthly revenue record of $220.7 million in October 2024. FanDuel and BetMGM lead the operator market, driving $41.5 million in tax revenue—a portion of which supports Detroit and tribal authorities.

Pennsylvania: High Stakes, High Rewards

Legalized in 2017, Pennsylvania ranks as a high-volume but high-barrier market for iGaming. October 2024 saw $189 million in revenue, primarily driven by Hollywood Casino at Penn National. Though licensing fees and tax rates are steep, the market remains highly profitable and continues to attract investment.

Connecticut: Steady Growth Since Legalization

Connecticut launched iGaming in 2021, including sports betting, poker, and casino games. The state posted a 44.7% year-over-year revenue increase in 2023, and hit a new record of $31.9 million in October 2024. DraftKings controls 60% of the market share, reinforcing strong operator dominance.

Rhode Island: New Entrant with Big Potential

Rhode Island joined the legal iGaming scene in 2024, expanding beyond sports betting into online casino games. Still in its early stages, the state is positioning itself for long-term growth through legislative foresight and a streamlined rollout strategy.

West Virginia: Small State, Strong Performance

Since launching online casinos in 2019, West Virginia has quietly built momentum. October 2024 marked a record $23.4 million in revenue, driven by popular platforms offering slots, table games, and online poker. With fewer regulatory hurdles, it offers an attractive entry point for new brands.
