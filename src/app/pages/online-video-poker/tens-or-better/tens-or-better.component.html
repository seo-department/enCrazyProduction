Understanding Tens or Better Video Poker Hands

  • Royal Flush: A hand containing an Ace. King, Queen, Jack, and 10 all of the same suit. This is the highest paying hand in Tens or Better Video Poker.
  • Four of a Kind: A hand containing four cards of the same value.
  • Straight Flush: A hand with 5 sequential cards of the same suit.
  • Flush: A hand with 5 cards of the same suit.
  • Straight: A hand containing five cards in sequential order, but it does not have to be the same suit.
  • Full House: A hand of a combination of a three of a kind and a pair.
  • Three of a Kind: A hand containing three cards of the same value.
  • Two Pairs: A hand with two of the same value cards.
  • Tens or Better: A hand with a pair of either 10s, Jacks, Kings, Queens, or Aces.

Strategy to Optimise Your Tens or Better Video Poker Game

  • Never break up a paying hand unless players have four of the five cards required to get a royal flush.
  • Low pairs (pairs lower than the 10s) are only worth playing if they are part of a full house. If the possibility of making a full house is lower than making a flush or straight, it is wiser to discard the low pairs.
  • Keep pairs higher than 10s when you have no other play options available.
  • Try to aim for a straight flush or a royal flush over a pair or straight.
  • Keep a four of a kind, especially if there are high cards.
  • Keep a straight, flush, full house, or three of a kind.

    • Keep two suited high cards.
    • Hold all three cards to a straight flush.
